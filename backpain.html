Gentle Poses to Ease Discomfort and Restore Alignment


Introduction

Yoga offers effective, non-invasive ways to manage and relieve back pain. Whether caused by poor posture, muscle imbalances, or sedentary habits, specific poses can help release tension, stretch tight areas, and build supportive strength in the core and spine. These calming, therapeutic postures are perfect for daily practice or as-needed relief.


Recommended Poses


  • Cat-Cow Stretch (Marjaryasana-Bitilasana) – mobilizes the spine and warms up back muscles
  • Child’s Pose (Balasana) – decompresses the spine and calms the nervous system
  • Knees-to-Chest (Apanasana) – gently stretches the lower back and relieves pressure
  • Supine Twist (Supta Matsyendrasana) – eases spinal tension and aids digestion

How to Practice


  • Use props like bolsters, blankets, and blocks to enhance support and relaxation
  • Focus on slow, deep breathing (such as diaphragmatic or box breathing) during each pose
  • Practice in a quiet, dim space to enhance the calming effect.
  • Stay in each pose for 3–10 minutes to experience full benefit.
  • Finish with Savasana or meditation for complete mental reset.

Why It Works

These poses work by gently lengthening the spine, releasing muscle tightness, and encouraging better posture. Movements like forward folds and supported backbends help decompress the vertebrae and soothe inflammation, while poses that activate the core and glutes build structural support for long-term relief.
