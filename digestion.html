Stimulate, Soothe, and Support the Gut


Introduction

Yoga can play a powerful role in supporting digestive health. Specific poses help stimulate the digestive organs, encourage natural movement (peristalsis), relieve bloating, and reduce gut-related tension. These gentle twists, compressions, and stretches support the body’s ability to digest, detoxify, and absorb nutrients — making them perfect after meals or as part of a regular wellness routine.


Recommended Poses


  • Seated Twist (Ardha Matsyendrasana) – massages abdominal organs and supports elimination
  • Cat-Cow Stretch (Marjaryasana-Bitilasana) – gently stimulates the digestive tract
  • Cobra Pose (Bhujangasana) – strengthens abdominal muscles and improves circulation
  • Bridge Pose (Setu Bandhasana) – promotes blood flow to the core and aids organ function

How to Practice


  • Practice on an empty stomach or at least 2 hours after eating
  • Focus on slow, deep breathing (such as diaphragmatic or box breathing) during each pose
  • Move slowly and breathe deeply into the belly to enhance effectiveness
  • Use gentle twists rather than forceful ones to avoid strain
  • Finish with Savasana or meditation for complete mental reset.

Why It Works

These poses apply light pressure and movement to the abdominal area, which helps awaken sluggish digestion and ease discomfort. Twists and compressions stimulate the liver and intestines, improving detox and nutrient flow, while forward folds calm the mind and reduce stress — a common factor in poor digestion
