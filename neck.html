Relax, Stretch, and Strengthen


Introduction

Your neck and shoulders carry more than just your head — they hold the stress of your day, poor posture, and long hours at a desk. Over time, this tension can lead to discomfort, stiffness, and even headaches. This section is designed to help you relieve pain, improve flexibility, and build strength in your neck and shoulders through safe, effective movements.


Recommended Poses


  • Plank Pose (Phalakasana) – strengthens arms, shoulders, core, and legs
  • Chair Pose (Utkatasana) – builds strength in thighs, glutes, and ankles
  • Warrior II (Virabhadrasana II) – tones legs, hips, and shoulders
  • Boat Pose (Navasana) – targets the deep core and hip flexors

How to Practice


  • Use props like bolsters, blankets, and blocks to enhance support and relaxation
  • Focus on slow, deep breathing (such as diaphragmatic or box breathing) during each pose
  • Practice in a quiet, dim space to enhance the calming effect.
  • Stay in each pose for 3–10 minutes to experience full benefit.
  • Finish with Savasana or meditation for complete mental reset.

Why It Works

These poses engage multiple muscle groups at once, using bodyweight as resistance to build strength without the need for equipment. The dynamic holds and transitions tone muscles by increasing time under tension, while improving joint stability and alignment.
