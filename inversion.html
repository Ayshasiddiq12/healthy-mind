Introduction

Inversion poses in yoga involve positioning the heart above the head, reversing the usual effects of gravity on the body. These poses range from gentle postures like Legs-Up-the-Wall to more advanced positions like Headstands and Handstands. Inversions stimulate circulation, activate the core, and offer a fresh mental and physical perspective. They are known for their revitalizing, therapeutic, and balancing qualities.

How to Practice


  • Start with a neutral spine either seated, standing, or lying down.
  • Inhale to lengthen your spine, creating space between vertebrae.
  • Exhale and gently twist, starting from your lower spine up through your shoulders and neck..
  • Keep your hips stable and twist with awareness — never force it..
  • Hold for 3–5 breaths, then slowly return to center and switch sides..

Cautions

Inversions are powerful and should be approached mindfully. Those with neck injuries, high blood pressure, glaucoma, or heart conditions should consult a professional before practicing advanced inversions. Beginners should use props or a wall for support and build strength gradually..
