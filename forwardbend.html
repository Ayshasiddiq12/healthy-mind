Surrender, Stretch, and Soothe


Introduction

Forward bend poses invite a sense of calm and introspection, combining deep physical stretching with emotional release. These postures gently lengthen the spine, hamstrings, and lower back while encouraging the mind to slow down. Whether seated or standing, forward bends symbolize surrender and grounding — ideal for relieving tension, fatigue, and stress.


Benefits


  • Calms the nervous system and reduces anxiety
  • Strengthens the back, shoulders, and glutes
  • Opens the chest and lungs, enhancing breath capacity
  • Encourages introspection and emotional release
  • Relieves tension in the lower back and neck
  • Boosts energy and counters fatigue or sluggishness
  • Encourages emotional release and confidence
  • Calms the nervous system, helping reduce stress
  • Improves Digestive Health
  • Stimulates digestive organs and improves circulation

How to Practice


  • Start with a neutral spine either seated, standing, or lying down.
  • Inhale to lengthen your spine, creating space between vertebrae.
  • Exhale and gently twist, starting from your lower spine up through your shoulders and neck..
  • Keep your hips stable and twist with awareness — never force it..
  • Hold for 3–5 breaths, then slowly return to center and switch sides..

Cautions

Forward bends should be approached gently, especially for those with tight hamstrings, sciatica, or lower back issues. Avoid rounding the spine excessively; instead, lengthen through the crown of the head. Use props or bend the knees as needed to support safe alignment.
