Unwind the Body, Calm the Mind


Introduction

Yoga can be a powerful evening ritual to prepare the body and mind for deep, restorative sleep. Gentle, calming poses relax the muscles, slow the breath, and signal the nervous system that it’s time to wind down. By releasing physical tension and mental restlessness, these postures create the perfect bridge between wakefulness and restful slumber.


Recommended Poses


  • Legs-Up-the-Wall Pose (Viparita Karani) – deeply relaxing; eases tension in legs and lower back
  • Child’s Pose (Balasana) – gently grounds the body and relieves mental fatigue
  • Reclining Bound Angle (Supta Baddha Konasana) – opens the hips while calming the nervous system
  • Seated Forward Fold (Paschimottanasana) – soothes the spine and encourages inward focus

How to Practice


  • Use props like bolsters, blankets, and blocks to enhance support and relaxation
  • Focus on slow, deep breathing (such as diaphragmatic or box breathing) during each pose
  • Practice in a quiet, dim space to enhance the calming effect.
  • Stay in each pose for 3–10 minutes to experience full benefit.
  • Finish with Savasana or meditation for complete mental reset.

Why It Works

These poses help quiet the mind and relax the body by activating the parasympathetic nervous system — the body’s natural “rest and digest” mode. Gentle stretching relieves physical tightness accumulated throughout the day, while the breath slows and deepens, lowering heart rate and blood pressure.
