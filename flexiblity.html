Open the Body, Expand Your Range of Motion


Introduction

Improving flexibility through yoga enhances mobility, reduces stiffness, and lowers the risk of injury. Regular stretching of muscles and connective tissue not only improves physical performance but also supports smoother movement in everyday life. These poses gently and progressively open the hips, hamstrings, shoulders, and spine — making flexibility accessible to all levels over time.


Recommended Poses


  • Downward-Facing Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana) – stretches the spine, hamstrings, and calves
  • Seated Forward Bend (Paschimottanasana) – lengthens the back and hamstrings
  • Low Lunge (Anjaneyasana) – opens the hips and quadriceps
  • Reclining Hand-to-Big-Toe Pose (Supta Padangusthasana) – targets hamstrings and calves

How to Practice


  • Use props like bolsters, blankets, and blocks to enhance support and relaxation
  • Focus on slow, deep breathing (such as diaphragmatic or box breathing) during each pose
  • Practice in a quiet, dim space to enhance the calming effect.
  • Stay in each pose for 3–10 minutes to experience full benefit.
  • Finish with Savasana or meditation for complete mental reset.

Why It Works

These poses slowly stretch key muscle groups, increasing circulation and elasticity in the fascia and joints. Over time, consistent practice trains the body to move more freely, with less tension and restriction..
